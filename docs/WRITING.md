# WRITING — MindWP

Authority for public-facing language, voice, rewrite behavior, banned phrasing, and copy validation. For a compact operational checklist, use `.agents/skills/mindwp-writing/SKILL.md`.

This doc controls how MindWP sounds. It does not decide identity, offer ownership, page roles, CTA strategy, or design.

Do not duplicate system definitions here. Use [STRATEGY.md](./STRATEGY.md) for system ownership and use this doc only to control public language, phrasing, tone, and copy validation.

## Use This Doc

Use this when writing, rewriting, polishing, or auditing public copy for:

- homepage sections
- service pages
- implementation service pages
- industry pages
- blog and resource pages
- case studies
- CTAs and contact context
- SEO-facing descriptions where public tone matters

Offer ownership is defined in [STRATEGY.md](./STRATEGY.md). Page roles are defined in [PAGES.md](./PAGES.md). CTA posture is defined in [PAGES.md](./PAGES.md).

## Writing Contract

Start with what the reader is experiencing, not what MindWP offers.

Write in:

```text
FLOW -> HANDLING -> RESULT
```

Do not default to:

```text
PAGES -> DESIGN -> FEATURES
SERVICES -> TOOLS -> FEATURES
```

Good MindWP writing feels observed. It sounds like someone watched the business for a day and noticed where work slipped away.

But recognition is the opening, not the whole page. **Reach the concrete offer and real proof quickly.** After the reader sees their situation, name what the smart website actually does and show the work — don't make them read an essay to find the offer.

Bad:

> We provide comprehensive digital solutions that streamline lead management.

Good:

> Calls come in while the team is on jobs. Forms sit in an inbox. Nobody is sure who followed up.

## Voice

The voice is:

- clear
- practical
- calm
- commercially serious
- direct without being aggressive
- warm without being soft
- operational rather than decorative
- specific before smooth

Use "we" and "you." Use contractions. Prefer short and medium sentences. Avoid polished marketing symmetry.

If a line sounds clever, impressive, or designed, simplify it.

## Non-Negotiable Rules

- Start with the buyer's real situation.
- Make important lines visible, audible, measurable, or recognisable.
- Keep one idea per sentence where possible.
- Protect strong authority lines; do not rewrite them just because they can be rephrased.
- Avoid generic agency, SaaS, AI chatbot, CRM reseller, ranking-hype, and tool-first language.
- Do not invent proof — no fabricated numbers, rankings, or results; the canonical "never invent proof" list lives in [STRATEGY.md](./STRATEGY.md).
- Do not revive removed service names or removed offer models.
- Do not publicly mention backend platform or white-label delivery names.
- Attach "yours / you own" to the website (their domain, content, enquiries, work, data) — **never to the handling/CRM**. The handling is a service MindWP runs; never write or imply "your CRM" or "a system you own." (Full rule: CRM Language below.)
- Treat Revenue Recovery as a layer/theme only, not a service page or offer category.
- Treat implementation services as Smart Website Systems implementation pathways.

## Rewrite Behavior

AI should behave like an editor protecting authority, not a rewriting engine.

Default sequence:

1. Read the full page or section.
2. Identify the weak parts.
3. Preserve strong lines and proven structure.
4. Rewrite only the approved scope.
5. Run copy validation.

Use a full rewrite only when the section or page is structurally broken, generic, contaminated by old strategy, or weak across most of its content.

During a full rewrite, existing content is context, not a phrasing source. Extract the meaning, discard the weak language, and rebuild around problem -> scenario -> outcome.

## Public Starting Points

Begin with working-day objects and moments:

- calls
- forms
- messages
- quotes
- bookings
- consultation requests
- appointments
- missed replies
- reviews
- completed jobs
- customers waiting
- owner memory
- scattered inboxes
- staff pressure
- local trust
- patient trust
- proof not collected
- follow-up nobody owns

If a section opens with internal language before the visible problem is clear, reverse the order.

Weak:

> Our connected handling system improves lead routing.

Stronger:

> The enquiry comes in. Nobody sees it quickly enough. By the time someone replies, the customer has moved on.

## Protected Positioning Anchors

Use these deliberately. Do not dilute them into weaker variants:

- "Work Comes In. Too Much Slips Away."
- "The business is working. The system around it is leaking."
- "These aren't separate problems. They're connected."
- "Nothing depends on someone remembering."
- "Enquiries land somewhere useful."

They should not appear everywhere. Repetition makes them feel like slogans instead of strategy.

## Words And Phrases To Avoid In Public Copy

Avoid generic filler unless there is a precise reason:

- leverage
- streamline
- seamless
- robust
- scalable
- cutting-edge
- powerful
- comprehensive
- holistic
- ecosystem
- touchpoint
- actionable insights
- growth machine
- all-in-one platform
- done-for-you marketing machine

Avoid public tool/vendor framing:

- AI chatbot as the offer
- AI receptionist as the whole product
- CRM platform setup
- automation platform
- workflow engine
- backend stack
- dashboard features as the main value
- ranking guarantees
- dominate Google
- cheap website packages

The word "system" is allowed. It is part of MindWP. Do not use it as filler where the sentence can name the actual business change.

The hard subset of banned terms is enforced by `check:names`; this doc owns the full human list and the rationale.

## Preferred Replacements

| Instead of             | Write                                                             |
| ---------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| lead capture           | their details land somewhere useful                               |
| CRM setup              | every enquiry has an owner and next step                          |
| automated reminders    | nobody has to chase                                               |
| AI chatbot             | fast response when someone reaches out                            |
| pipeline management    | you can see where each enquiry stands                             |
| reputation software    | good work becomes visible proof                                   |
| SEO rankings           | nearby customers can find and verify you                          |
| automation workflow    | follow-up happens without chasing                                 |
| comprehensive tracking | you can see what happened                                         |
| website redesign       | rebuild the website around clarity, trust, and connected handling |

Use replacements as direction, not mandatory exact copy.

## Paid Attention Language

Paid attention is still just attention. Use this idea to protect the MindWP boundary, not to create an ads offer.

Safe language:

- "Paid attention is still just attention."
- "However the enquiry arrives, it still needs somewhere useful to land."
- "The source changes. The handling problem does not."
- "More attention does not fix a vague page, a buried enquiry path, or a slow first response."
- "A paid click, a referral, a call, a message, and an organic visit can all leak when the next step is unclear."
- "MindWP does not run campaigns; it makes sure attention has a clear website-and-handling path once it reaches the business."

Avoid:

- "We fix your ads."
- "Improve ROAS."
- "Lower cost per lead."
- "Turn paid clicks into bookings."
- "Campaign performance system."
- "Facebook ads funnel."
- guaranteed leads, bookings, rankings, revenue, conversion lift, or campaign results
- language that makes MindWP sound like a media buyer, ads manager, campaign optimisation service, or analytics product

Use the doctrine selectively. It belongs where the page already discusses website clarity, enquiry landing, response, ownership, follow-up, or proof. Do not bend an industry page, proof section, or service-page flagship away from its owned business moment just to mention paid clicks.

## Website System Language

When writing about website systems, lead with what changes:

- the offer becomes easier to understand
- service, treatment, or procedure pages make the next step clear
- trust signals appear where decisions happen
- calls, forms, messages, bookings, and consultation requests connect into a handling path
- the website becomes a visible control point
- the website works as part of the business instead of sitting alone

Avoid:

- pretty site language
- page-count packages
- generic custom website claims
- design-only portfolios
- builder-first phrasing

Wrong:

> We build beautiful websites for service businesses.

Right:

> The website explains the work clearly, builds trust before the call, and connects enquiries into a handling path someone owns.

For specialist clinics and private practices, the website should be treated as the practice front door. It should help patients understand the service or treatment, trust the provider, choose the right next step, and move into a booking or consultation path without turning the page into medical software, EMR, compliance, or treatment-claim language.

Useful test:

> A website should help the business bring in and handle enquiries properly.

Do not use that as mandatory final copy. Use it as a test: if the page only talks about design, pages, technology, or traffic, it is missing the MindWP point.

## Connected Handling Language

Connected handling is public language for response, routing, ownership, follow-up, review requests, proof capture, and recovery around the website.

Connected handling must be earned. Do not open a page or section with the phrase before the buyer recognises the working-day problem. First show the calls, forms, messages, quotes, follow-up, reviews, or ownership gaps. Then use connected handling to name the path around the website.

Use language like:

- the right person finds out
- missed calls move into a response path
- follow-up has an owner
- quotes do not depend on memory
- review requests happen at the right time
- good work becomes proof
- consultation requests are followed up
- patient experience becomes visible proof

Do not make the buyer learn backend architecture before they understand the business problem.

## AI Language

AI may support delivery, but AI is not the product.

Lead with:

- fast response
- missed-call handling
- routing
- after-hours response
- fewer enquiries disappearing

Avoid:

- AI chatbot hype
- AI replacing the business
- AI as the hero offer
- tool-demo language

Good:

> Missed calls and forms move into a response path instead of disappearing.

Bad:

> Our AI chatbot handles your customer enquiries.

## CRM Language

CRM may support delivery, but the public problem is ownership and follow-up.

"CRM" may appear in the system name, but public copy must not lead with software. Explain the business change first: owner, status, next step, follow-up, and visibility. Mention CRM structure only when it helps the buyer understand how those things stay visible.

**The ownership boundary (load-bearing).** The _website_ is the client's — they own it and it stays theirs; attach "yours / you own" to the website, their domain, content, enquiries, work, and data. The _connected handling (the CRM)_ is **not** theirs to own — it's a service MindWP sets up and runs. Never write or imply "your CRM," "a system you own," or "run your pipeline" — anything that hands the handling to the client to own or operate quietly converts the thing we deliberately _don't_ sell (the backend, run by us) into something they own and run. Say instead: _run for you_ / _we keep it running_ / _you're not left to feed it_. The CRM may have an owner per enquiry ("who owns the next step") — that's the _enquiry's_ owner, not the client owning the system. Model copy: the SWS FAQ — "The website is yours… the connected handling is a service we run for you."

Lead with:

- every enquiry has an owner
- every enquiry has a status
- next steps are visible
- follow-up does not depend on memory
- quotes are not left sitting after they are sent

Quote and estimate boundary: MindWP does not create, prepare, price, invoice, automatically send, or send quotes. The business prepares or sends the quote; Follow-Up & CRM keeps the owner, status, next step, reminder, follow-up, and decision visibility clear around it. Prefer language such as "estimate requested," "estimate discussed," "team-prepared quote needs follow-up," "quote follow-up due," "decision pending," or "waiting on customer." Avoid copy and artifact states that make MindWP look like quote or estimating software.

Avoid:

- platform setup as the product
- CRM feature lists
- internal pipeline jargon before the problem is visible

## SEO Language

Local SEO language should sound like find, verify, and trust.

Use:

- nearby customers can find you
- service areas are clear
- local trust signals support the decision
- Google Business Profile and website signals line up
- traffic has clear service pages and next steps to land on
- visibility is maintained over time, not installed once

Avoid:

- guaranteed rankings
- domination language
- secret tricks
- traffic-only claims
- treating Google Business Profile as the whole system

Useful concepts to preserve:

- Local SEO starts with the website.
- Traffic only helps when services and next steps are clear.
- If the website does not build trust, local traffic leaks.
- If service-area relevance is unclear, visibility is weaker.

These are writing principles, not slogans to repeat on every page.

## Reviews Language

Review language should sound like proof, feedback, and timing.

Use:

- good work becomes visible proof
- review requests happen after the right moment
- feedback routes to the right place
- public trust reflects completed work
- patient experience becomes visible proof where clinic context is approved

Avoid:

- guaranteed star ratings
- fake reviews
- review manipulation
- reputation software as the product

## Implementation Service Language

WordPress, Elementor, Bricks, Divi, WooCommerce, and rebuild pages are implementation pathways under Smart Website Systems.

Lead with:

- website clarity
- service-page clarity
- trust
- enquiry capture
- maintainable editing where relevant
- performance where relevant
- connected handling around response, follow-up, reviews, and proof

Do not lead with:

- builder fandom
- cheap packages
- page-count pricing
- template customization
- plugin stacks
- platform comparison for its own sake
- feature-driven builds disconnected from the business problem

Good:

> The builder matters because the website has to stay clear, fast, editable, and connected to the handling path around it.

Bad:

> We build stunning Elementor websites for any business.

Implementation copy should repel looks-only redesign buyers, cheapest-website shoppers, and generic builder-service shoppers. It should attract buyers who want a serious website system that supports clarity, trust, enquiry capture, and connected handling.

## Case Study Tone

Case studies should sound observed, not polished.

They should show:

- what was happening
- what was broken
- what changed
- what became easier to see or handle
- what outcome is real, measured, or observable

Do not imply fictional or constructed material is real client work. Do not invent names, ratings, reviews, review counts, dates, locations, testimonials, metrics, rankings, revenue, attribution, or before/after results.

Use honest evidence labels when a Work item, case study, demonstration build, anonymised client item, or scenario could be mistaken for real client proof or measured outcomes. Do not force defensive labels such as "illustrative," "sample," "example," "demo," or "mockup" onto obvious service-page artifacts — page anatomy, review documents, work records, setup notes, editorial proof objects, SVG scenes, and neutral internal diagrams — when the form already reads as constructed.

Avoid "Results," "ROI," "Testimonial," "What it cost and returned," and fake before/after framing unless the page is a real case study with real evidence.

Use constraints, tradeoffs, ordinary operational details, and client language where available.

## Industry Specificity

Industry pages must feel specific because the working conditions are specific.

Specificity is not adding the industry name to generic copy. It is showing:

- timing pressure
- decision speed
- customer or patient behavior
- trust concerns
- enquiry patterns
- quote, booking, appointment, or consultation patterns
- review/proof expectations

**Focus on scheduled, considered work — not emergencies.** MindWP targets planned jobs: quotes, surveys, estimates, consultations, installs, replacements, and the follow-up over the days and weeks while the buyer decides. Do **not** lead with emergency, urgent, same-day, "burst pipe / no heat / storm-tonight" framing, or any within-the-hour/within-minutes response promise. The leak MindWP fixes is the _considered_ enquiry that goes quiet — the quote nobody followed up, the consultation request that waited, the survey that was never booked.

So: roofing is a re-roof or repair the homeowner is getting quotes for, not a storm emergency. HVAC is an install, replacement, or service booked at a convenient time, not a no-heat call-out. Clinics are consultation-led, considered decisions (implants, orthodontics, dermatology) where trust builds over time. The realities that should shape the copy are quote follow-up, consultation follow-up, booking confidence, decision time, and proof — not urgency.

When using time language, confirm whether the time belongs to the buyer's decision or to MindWP's handling. Do not turn a considered decision period into a promise that MindWP manages the whole period.

## Section Rhythm

A useful underlying arc — **not a rigid template to stamp on every page:**

```text
Recognition -> Friction -> Clarity (the offer) -> Proof -> Action
```

This is a guide for where copy energy goes, not a fixed section list or order. The conversion arc itself is owned by [STRATEGY.md](./STRATEGY.md); WRITING owns only how copy energy maps onto it. Pages should vary their structure and silhouette using `.agents/skills/mindwp-design/SKILL.md` — applying the identical rhythm to every page is exactly what made the old site feel templated. What every page must do is reach the **offer** and show real **proof** before the strong CTA; how it gets there can differ.

Hero sections create recognition. They should not teach the whole system.

Problem sections show real situations. They should not jump straight to the offer.

Comparison sections show operating contrast, not mirrored benefit bullets.

Proof sections show evidence, scenario, or observed change. They should not generalize.

CTA sections should feel like a practical diagnostic conversation. CTA rules live in [PAGES.md](./PAGES.md); the approved label strings are owned by `lib/cta`.

Control repetition at page level. Do not make every section explain the full mechanism with the same noun cluster. Give each section one rhetorical job, then distribute language accordingly: buyer behaviour, trust, contradiction, confirmation, maintenance, payoff, fit, objection, or action. The full practical cluster belongs mainly in the scope/process section; earlier and later sections should use lighter role-specific language instead of repeating the whole list.

If an artifact explains the mechanism, surrounding copy should frame it, not repeat it. Repeated support lists that restate the artifact usually signal that the section needs clearer hierarchy, not more explanation.

Primary service pages must not all repeat the full MindWP model. Each service page should own one business moment and use the wider connected path only where it clarifies that page's problem. If every service page says website + response + follow-up + reviews + recovery in the same way, the pages have blurred.

Avoid:

- public infrastructure language as the lead idea
- automation as a visible homepage pillar
- AI as a named public capability
- workflow language everywhere
- generic lead-management wording
- technology cards as filler
- systems explained as a stack before the reader recognises the problem

## Offer, Proof, And Pricing Language

Offer, proof, and pricing carry the sell. Commercial detail lives in [STRATEGY.md](./STRATEGY.md); this is how to _say_ it.

**Offer.** State plainly what the buyer gets. The smart website is the website _and_ the handling built into it — it brings work in and makes sure it doesn't slip away. Lead with the difference, not the philosophy:

> Good: Most websites stop at the contact form. Ours carries the enquiry the whole way through — answered, owned, followed up, and turned into proof.
> Weak: We deliver connected handling across the customer lifecycle.

**Proof.** Point at real work, never invented results. Talk about what's _visible_ in a build, not numbers that aren't real:

> Good: A finished roofing site we built — clear service areas, the quote path in view, reviews placed beside the decision.
> Banned: Increased leads 240%. Rated 4.9 by 312 customers.

Label demonstration builds, anonymised work, and client work honestly when they are being used as evidence (see [PAGES.md](./PAGES.md)). Do not over-label obvious constructed service-page artifacts. Founder authority is stated modestly and truthfully: "shipping service-business websites since 2015," "a small senior team." No faces, no fabricated bios.

**Pricing.** A calm scope/price _posture_, not a tier wall. Help the buyer self-qualify on budget without pressure:

> Good: Fixed-scope, from a clear starting point — you'll know what's included before we start.
> Avoid: Plans from $99/mo! · Cheapest websites in town · custom quote required (hidden).

## Numbers And Claims

Numbers are allowed only when their type is clear:

- sourced benchmark
- clearly framed diagnostic estimate
- real measured result

Not allowed:

- invented client results
- guaranteed outcomes
- fabricated rankings
- unsupported revenue claims
- review guarantees

If it is not real measured proof, do not present it as proof.

## Copy Validation

Before approving public copy, confirm:

- the reader's situation appears before MindWP's offer
- the language feels practical and observable
- the page follows the active offer ownership from [STRATEGY.md](./STRATEGY.md)
- the page role matches [PAGES.md](./PAGES.md)
- the CTA tone matches [PAGES.md](./PAGES.md)
- no removed service names or old offer structures appear
- no backend platform names appear publicly
- AI, CRM, SEO, and reviews are framed by outcomes, not tools
- no fake proof or unsupported claims appear
- clinic copy does not make unsupported medical, treatment, compliance, or patient-result claims
- implementation pages resolve upward to Smart Website Systems
- Revenue Recovery does not appear as a service, offer category, CTA category, page theme, or public promise

Final rule: protect authority first, improve language second.
